Trivial Graph Features and Old-School Learning for Anomalous Link Detection in Link Streams

Abstract:

Detecting anomalies in link streams that represent various kinds of interactions is an important research topic with crucial applications. However, most proposed methods rely on complex approaches that raise computational and/or interpretability issues. In addition, because of the lack of ground truth data, they are mostly evaluated on injected anomalies. In contrast with these works, we show here that trivial graph features and old-school learning techniques are sufficient to detect such anomalies extremely well. In addition, this basic approach has very low computational costs and it leads to easily interpretable results. It also has many other desirable properties that we study through an extensive set of experiments. Since most research works in the area are evaluated on random link injections, much effort is devoted to the optimization of detection in such cases. Our work closes this research direction. Detection methods should now target more complex kinds of anomalies, which requires advanced anomaly models and/or publicly available ground-truth data.
